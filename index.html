Workflow

What is workflow?

The movement of documents around an organization for purposes including sign-off, evaluation, performing activities in a process and co-writing.
Webster's Dictionary

Another more abstract take

Workflow comes down to just two ideas: that there is work to do, which a workflow sees as opaque units of behavior; and flow, which describes what work gets done when
Dave Green

University Policy Office: Case Study

Who is UPO?

The University Policy Office at The University of Texas at Austin

A new Compliance Services office,

supported by the Policy Office Advisory Group,

working closely with President's Office and Legal Services.

What does UPO do?

  1. Create / maintain Handbook of Operating Procedures.
  2. Record history of changes, approvals, and effective dates.
  3. Guide documents through a complex review process engaging policy owners, sponsors, subject matter experts, and advisors.
  4. Assimilate stores of University policy outside of the HOP
  5. Provide historical policy materials for dispute resolution.

What did UPO need?

  1. Unify and expedite a disjointed review process under a new workflow model
  2. Reference policy revisions, related documentation, and effective dates
  1. Manage and track policy ownership and sponsorship
  2. Engage authors and moderators with minimal administrative overhead

Highly variable workflow

Overview

Flow from creation, review, through publication of policies and supplements varies widely case-by-case.

Workbench Moderation allows limitless node states BUT:

DoR Transitions:

  • Transitions managed individually
  • Transitions do not control access → twice the maintenance
  • Moderation history damaged if revisions are deleted
  • Transition steps (approvals) require node editing access

Workbench Access provides taxonomy-based access BUT:

DoR Access Sections:

  • Traditional Access models are based on static relationships to nodes (categories, business areas, etc.)
  • Recommended permission settings are over permissive

“Moderating” with Access

UPO Access Groups

  • Taxonomy-based Workbench Access scheme mirrored UPO constituents
  • Role permissions + taxonomy traversal → capabilities are as-needed
  • Few, simple Moderation states: Draft, In Review, Published

Using Moderation states to trigger, not govern, the workflow

UPO Moderation Transitions

  • New nodes and edits on pre-existing nodes start in Draft state
  • Authors trigger review process by transitioning to In Review.
  • Using the state change hook (API) we triggered the creation of our “helper” workflow node.

Controlling Workflow on its side: Workflow Checklist nodes

Workflow Checklist

  • Helper nodes tied to documents in review
  • Recorded starting and ending VIDs
  • Enabled comments on helper nodes with extended commenting form to record routing
  • Instructions and responses recorded in comment body for rich moderation experience and history.

Challenge Solved

The flow of creation and publication of policies and supplemental documentation varies case-by-case.

Rotate Workbench: moderate with Access, use helper nodes, use state transitions permissions to manage workflow triggers not direct workflow.

Complex Permission Requirements

Overview

Policy editing capabilities need tight management, draft visibility should make business sense, managing access and permissions should be simple, workflow tasks must be delegate-able.

Moderation: Recommended configuration didn't work here

Recommended permission settings for moderation posed management, security, and business challenges.

  • No concept of a non-editing reviewer editors, moderators and publishers all need "edit any content type"
  • Review roles can see all moderation messages but policy is a sensitive/legal space
  • Users with multiple roles have too much power in some capacities
  • Suited to linear and hierarchical flow/access scheme - not as-needed variable workflow

Access: Other issues

  • Administration of access can be opaque - needs to be dead simple
  • Need to honor and protect policy portfolios and allow delegation within portfolios
  • Taxonomy traversal prevented access by policy stakeholders when out of their taxonomy

Solving Permission Problems Differently

  • Did not grant "edit any content"
  • Custom CTOOLS-based access plugins that interpreted taxonomy traversal
    • Augmented workbench node access restrictions with taxonomy traversal sensitive plugin.
    • As a result, nodes in unpublished state in workflow could now be seen by non-editors
    • Also used for panels pane visibility and restricting auxiliary node access in relation to taxonomy traversal
    • Used outside of CTOOLs spaces by defining contexts and mocking CTOOLs
    • Combined with taxonomy references on policy nodes provided taxonomy access exceptions for stakeholders

* Workbench Moderation 2 has switched to a CTOOLs based permission handling.

Solving Permission Problems Differently

Simplified permission management

  • Chose a proliferation of taxonomy terms over a proliferation of Roles and permissions
  • Permission changes within roles would be rare - reducing error
  • Add individuals to taxonomy terms by the user not role
  • Taxonomy is organized by role but empty at that level, child taxonomy terms house users and their delegates
  • Nodes have taxonomy references for sponsor and owner so that all members of a taxonomy can be delegates.

Overview

Policy editing capabilities need tight management, draft visibility should make business sense, managing access and permissions should be simple, workflow tasks must be delegatable.

Chose more restrictions through custom permissions and node access augmentation, simplified management through a proliferation of terms over roles, created workbench taxonomy term references to allow key term groups oversight capabilities on policy nodes.
