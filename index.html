Workflow

What is workflow?

The movement of documents around an organisation for purposes including sign-off, evaluation, performing activities in a process and co-writing.
Webster's Dictionary

University Policy Office: Case Study

Who is UPO?

The University Policy Office at The University of Texas at Austin

A new office within Office of Compliance,

supported by the Policy Office Advisory Group,

working closely with President's Office and Leagal Services.

What does UPO do?

  1. Create / maintain Handbook of Operating Procedures.
  2. Record history of changes, approvals, and effective dates of Policy Documents therein.
  3. Guide documents through a complex review process engaging policy owners, sponsors, subject matter experts, and advisors.
  4. Gather and compile legacy stores of University policy.
  5. Provide accurate history for dispute resolution.

What does UPO need?

  1. Structural and visual overhaul of HOP web presentation
  2. Escape from paper-based workflows and disjointed electronic tools
  3. Ability to easily index document history for context and reference
  4. Increased review efficiency to decrease review cycle length (years)
  5. Retention of meta-data and ownership of Policy Documents
  6. Easily engage POAG members with minimal administrative overhead

Chosing Drupal

Why Drupal?

  • Robust content modeling
  • Unify workflow application and content management
  • Abandon deeply disappointing proprietary CMS
  • Cost effectiveness

Chosing Drupal as CMS → Drupal for workflow

Updated public site generated “buy-in” from stakeholders

Experience in management of published content natually led to management of “pre-published” content.

Previous experience with Workbench led us to believe it was well suited to this task as well…

Enter the Workbench

What is Workbench?

Workbench provides overall improvements for managing content that Drupal does not provide out of the box.
Workbench Project Page

What does Workbench do?

Moderation

Customizable, multi-stepped revision and approval workflow for publishing content.

Access

Access based on editorial sections taken from hierarchical orgianizational structure.

Challenges & Solutions

Highly variable workflow

Creation, review, and publication of policies and supplemental documentation varies case-by-case.

Designed “routing sheet” content type, the Workflow Checklist, to record this traffic.

“My Workbench” dashboard inadequate



Workbench provided dashboard is meant for linear progress up to final publication for individual authors.

Designed Policy Dashboard with group- and role-specific views of drafted, published, and in-review documents.

Needed full history of policies



All review cycles, revisions, and status dates must be recorded and easily referenced (including pre-Workbench).

Active Policy Locator and HOP History views were built to display this history. Necessary metadata is recorded consistently and was extrapolated on installation.

Delegate participation



Many Policy Sponsors and some Policy Owners are department leaders who will want to delegate site usage.

Implemented Review Group, a taxonomy of user groups to which documents could be assigned.

Owners and Sponsors need to oversee documents



Even when documents are outside of an Owner's or Sponsor's review group, they need access.

Used custom CTools Access to restore draft-view and draft-edit permissions based on node Review Group associations.

Many Review Groups are read-only

Workbench assumes anyone with access to view a draft can edit it. Most groups should not have edit access; they review and comment.

Custom Node Access checks were written to restrict editing to Owners, Legal Services, and UPO Admins.

Policies are replacable



Multiple Policy Document nodes may be substituted in/out of the HOP for a specific record.

Book Module structures HOP Sections and Chapters with a simple node type. Policy nodes are matched as subpages of Chapters, ordered by weight. All HOP-specific views are aware.

Time-limits on review period

POAG (and others?) should be given an automatically enforced deadline.

Optional deadline can specified for any document movement. Cron will trigger a Workbench Access change to return the document to UPO.

Policy of Truth Summary

“Workflow” is a generic business term for an editorial process.

Stanford DoR and Texas UPO needed a way to manage this process within their CMS.

Workbench extends Drupal's content modeling with granulated revision management.

Novel ideas and deep business knowledge tackle challenges.
