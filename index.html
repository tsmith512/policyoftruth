Workflow

What is workflow?

The movement of documents around an organisation for purposes including sign-off, evaluation, performing activities in a process and co-writing.
Webster's Dictionary

Another more abstract take

Workflow comes down to just two ideas: that there is work to do, which a workflow sees as opaque units of behavior; and flow, which describes what work gets done when
Dave Green

University Policy Office: Case Study

Who is UPO?

The University Policy Office at The University of Texas at Austin

A new Compliance Services office,

supported by the Policy Office Advisory Group,

working closely with President's Office and Leagal Services.

What does UPO do?

  1. Create / maintain Handbook of Operating Procedures.
  2. Record history of changes, approvals, and effective dates.
  3. Guide documents through a complex review process engaging policy owners, sponsors, subject matter experts, and advisors.
  4. Assimilate stores of University policy outside of the HOP
  5. Provide historical policy materials for dispute resolution.

What did UPO need?

  1. Unify and expedite a disjointed review process under a new workflow model
  2. Reference policy revisions, related documentation, and effective dates
  1. Manage and track policy ownership and sponsorship
  2. Engage authors and moderators with minimal administrative overhead

Enter the Workbench

What is Workbench?

Workbench provides overall improvements for managing content that Drupal does not provide out of the box.
Workbench Project Page

What does Workbench do?

Moderation

Customizable revision and approval workflow with states between unpublished and published.

Access

Node access based on permissions and membership in a hierarchical taxonomy or menu system.

Highly variable workflow

Overview

The flow of creation, review, and publication of policies and supplemental documentation varies widely case-by-case.

Workbench Moderation gives you limitless node states BUT:

  • State transitions must be managed
  • Transitions don't control access - so twice the maintenance
  • Due to moderation history can't be deleted willy nilly
  • Moderation approval steps require node editing access

Workbench Access gives you taxonomy based node access BUT:

  • Traditional workbench access models are based on static relationships to nodes
  • Recommended permission settings are over permissive for a flow like this

So... we turned workflow on its side: “Moderating” with Access

Traditional workflow: A flow of actions taken by actors

Workflow on its side: A flow of actors who take actions

“Moderating” with Access

  • Combined role permissions and taxonomy that enabled user capabilities when a node entered their taxonomy
  • Used a simple series of states: draft, in review, published
  • Set up a taxonomy based workbench access scheme that mirrored UPO constituents

Using Moderation states to trigger, not govern, the worfklow

  • New nodes and edits on pre-existing nodes start in draft state (outside of workflow)
  • Authors trigger review process when they transition nodes to "in review" state
  • Using the workbench state change hook we triggered the creation of our helper a helper workflow node.

Controling Workflow on its side: Workflow nodes

  • Helper nodes that were tied to nodes in workflow
  • Record starting and ending VIDs
  • Workflow Nodes used an extended commenting form to record routing
  • Actions needed and responses were recorded as well which provides a far richer moderation experience and history.

Overview

The flow of creation, review, and publication of policies and supplemental documentation varies case-by-case.

Turn workflow on its side - moderate through access, use helper nodes, use state transitions permissions to manage workflow triggers not direct workflow.
